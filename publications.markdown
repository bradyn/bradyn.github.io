I have published six peer-reviewed papers in leading finance and economics
journals. My work has been cited over 300 times. These papers
develop and analyze mathematical models that address practical
questions about market behavior: how do prices
form when market participants have different information? How do
strategic decisions about information acquisition and disclosure
reshape market outcomes? How does feedback between market prices and real
investment decisions change firm behavior?

---

**[Asymmetric Information, Disagreement, and the Valuation of Debt and Equity](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2025.103995)** (with Snehal Banerjee and Kevin Smith)  
_Journal of Financial Economics_, 2025

How do private information and differences of opinion among investors
affect the relative pricing of a firm's debt versus equity?

**[Feedback Effects and Systematic Risk Exposures](https://doi.org/10.1111/jofi.13427)** (with Snehal Banerjee and Kevin Smith)  
_Journal of Finance_, 2025

How does the feedback between a firm's stock price and its investment
decisions change when the project is exposed to systematic risks, such
as climate risk?

**[On the Voluntary Disclosure of Redundant Information](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jet.2023.105743)** (with Snehal Banerjee, Ron Kaniel, and Ilan Kremer)  
_Journal of Economic Theory_, 2023

Why would a firm voluntarily disclose information that will eventually
be revealed anyway? Early disclosure changes how investors trade and
acquire information, ultimately improving long-term prices.

**[Dynamics of Research and Strategic Trading](https://doi.org/10.1093/rfs/hhab029)** (with Snehal Banerjee)  
_Review of Financial Studies_, 2022

When a strategic trader's decision to enter a market and begin
researching an asset is publicly observable, how does that affect
trading dynamics and price formation?

**[Strategic Trading and Unobservable Information Acquisition](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2020.05.007)** (with Snehal Banerjee)  
_Journal of Financial Economics_, 2020

How does a market operate when participants can secretly invest in
acquiring private information, and the market maker cannot observe
whether or how much they have learned?

**[On Existence and Uniqueness of Equilibrium in a Class of Noisy Rational Expectations Models](https://doi.org/10.1093/restud/rdv012)**  
_Review of Economic Studies_, 2015 | [Online Appendix](/assets/appendix-res2015.pdf)

A general mathematical framework for analyzing how prices aggregate and transmit
private information in markets, extending standard linear models to
accommodate a much broader class of nonlinear settings.
